Portfolio

Math Timed Test

Math Timed Test is an iPad app designed to help the younger students at my school to learn and be assessed on their addition, subtraction, and multiplication math facts. The app was my first assignment on an independent project for Porter-Gaud's Computer Science I course. Written completely in Swift, the app is available for free on the iOS App Store.

PG Schedule

I have contributed to two iterations of the Porter-Gaud Student Schedule app. The first was taken from a graduated senior's Computer Science IV project, and my task was to take it from "working condition" to an app which the entire school could use. The second iteration stood alongside the original, mainly being created to provide an API so future iOS and Watch apps can take advantage of it. The former is written in Javascript, while the latter was written in Node.js taking advantage of the MEAN stack (Mongo, Express, Angular, and Node). The second one is open source (available here) so other students can contribute and tweak the API to their liking.

WWDC Scholarship

I have won two WWDC Scholarships, in 2014 and 2015. The Scholarship program itself allowed for a number (200 in 2014, 350 in 2015) students to attend WWDC with a free conference ticket. Throughout both years, I have been an active member in our Slack and Facebook groups designed for us to communicate with each other. While the 2014 app was written in Objective-C, the 2015 version was written entirely in Swift, Apple's new programming language. The 2015 app is open source, available here on Github.

PG Progress Reports

For my second project in 9th grade Computer Science I, I created a website written in PHP to help teachers send progress reports to student's and their parents. Instead of manually gathering data about a student (parents, homeroom teacher, etc.), all that they are required to input in this app is the student's name and their own name. From there, the app automatically finds the names and emails of the student's parents, homeroom teacher, the principal, and the dean. The app seamlessly works between Middle and High school students, filling the appropriate dean/principal information for each one. The main page also features a typeahead (written with jQuery and Javascript) to filter the names of students and teachers.

WWDC Alerts

Before WWDC 2015 was announced by Apple, I wrote an app in Node (also using the MEAN stack) to notify anyone who signed up for the service as soon as the WWDC info page was updated. The app worked perfectly, and at 8:45 EST on April 14, dozens of people recieved an Twilio SMS message. The app was written after school one day, partially thanks to Mountain Dew and some pizza.

I wrote a second Node app after submissions were due to send a Yo notification to users as soon as their WWDC scholarship status email arrived. The app checked the Gmail API to see if the email had arrived, and would activate if it did. The core functionality was hacked together in just a night, and Philips Hue integration was added the next day. The Hue integration blinked the home's lights green or red based if the user was accepted or not. The entire app is open source on Github.
